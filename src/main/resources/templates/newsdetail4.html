Walt Disney will lay off 32,000 employees

32,000 employees will retire at the end of March 2021 when Covid-19 continues to damage Disney's amusement parks and resorts.

The dismissal plan was disclosed in the filing to the US Securities and Exchange Commission on November 25. Thus, the new plan has increased by 4,000 positions to be cut from the number 28,000 announced in September 2020. According to the most recent annual report, Disney employs about 223,000 employees.

Disney also warned that it could be forced to stop future dividends, reduce or not pay retirement and medical retirement payments. The company may also cut investments in television and movie products, and increase layoffs or lay off more employees.

"Some of these measures could have a negative impact on our business," Disney stated in the filing.

The pandemic severely affected Disney's park business, with more than 100,000 employees in the United States. The company also had to stop the cruise ship business and postpone release of major movies, such as "Black Widow", which is expected to be one of the biggest blockbusters of the year.

All 12 Disney parks in North America, Asia, and Europe closed from March to May. After that, Disney reopened its theme parks in Shanghai and Florida, but the storefront Their heads in California will remain closed at least until the end of 2020. Disneyland Paris is forced to close again late last month, when France imposed a second nationwide shutdown.

One bright spot for the company was online TV service Disney +, which now has 74 million subscribers and has become a focus of business after overhauling its media and entertainment division. CEO Bob Chapek said last week that running this business is "key" to the company's future.

Disney lost $ 2.8 billion in the fiscal year to September 30, in stark contrast to the previous year, when the company posted a profit of $ 10.4 billion.
