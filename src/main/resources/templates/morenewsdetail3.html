Republicans want President Biden to strongly respond to China's embargo

2021-01-17: 3 hours ago

Republican lawmakers pressured and demanded that President Joe Biden's government take a stronger response after China embargoed 28 former officials in its predecessor, Donald Trump.

China's Ministry of Foreign Affairs on January 20 announced imposing embargo on 28 former Republican Trump administration officials, including former Secretary of State Mike Pompeo, on the grounds of their "crazy" policies against China's interests, according to Reuters.

In addition to Pompeo, China's embargo list includes former trade adviser Peter Navarro, former national security adviser Robert O'Brien and former health minister Alex Azar.

On Twitter January 21, Republican Senator from the US Senate Foreign Relations Committee, Mr. Jim Risch said that Beijing "is challenging the new government's determination to maintain a tougher policy towards China. ". "Together, the Republicans and Democrats must show Beijing that we will not be discouraged in protecting American interests," said Risch.

In addition, Michael McCaul, top Republican congressman at the House Foreign Affairs Committee, said the embargo was a "brazen and unfounded attempt to silence and intimidate" officials. has exposed Beijing's acts of coercion and human rights violations.

Under the embargo, 28 former US officials and family members will be banned from entering mainland China, Hong Kong and Macau. These 28 companies and organizations are also prohibited from doing business with China. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China criticized these former officials for their "crazy" actions and policies to intervene in internal affairs, undermining China's interests and seriously affecting the second relationship. country.

Beijing introduced a daily embargo on the day Mr. Biden took office January 20. Before that, in the final days of his term, the Trump government pushed hard against China on many issues, including trade, security, technology , and the origin of the Covid-19 virus. and human rights.

On January 21, US National Security Council spokesman Emily Horne criticized China's embargo as futile and intended to cause division among political parties in the United States, but has not yet given a specific response. .

However, the Biden government is expected to hold a tough stance on China, according to Reuters. In a Senate hearing on January 20, President Biden's candidate for the position of foreign minister, Antony Blinken, said that while he disagreed completely, he believed that President Trump was right to take the hard approach. more solid with China.

Mr. Blinken noted that China poses the biggest challenge to the US and believes there is a very solid foundation for building a bipartisan policy against Beijing.
