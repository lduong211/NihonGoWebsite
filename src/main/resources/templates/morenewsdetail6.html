Thiago Alcantara will do Liverpool's beat-up attack: Play for funs!

2021-01-17: 3 hours ago

In general, Hamann thinks that Thiago 's slow technique will delay the pace of a Liverpool that everyone knows is always attacking.

With the same idea, the pen world is more "beautiful": Liverpool's "heavy metal" football will be turned into "slow rock" by Thiago!

Let's ask the reverse: music should just be "heavy metal" to be the standard? In contrast the other behind. You have the right to like, depending on the "taste" of each person, but it is never a standard thing for people to aim for. "Heavy metal" believers are definitely not the majority of music lovers.

In life, sometimes "good but not new" ideas, or "new but not good", are ... useless. Hamann's opinion sounded good at first. Unfortunately, it's 30 years old.

In the past, England had Glenn Hoddle - a rare talent favoring the technical style produced by his native football (Hamann was only a 6-year-old boy when Hoddle wore England for the first time). Hoddle that passes must be "unique" and correct. With his excellent ball holding technique, he always looks up before passing. The situation is not "delicious", then Hoddle continues to keep the ball, waiting for his teammates to move to a more favorable situation before passing.

In the 1980s, English football was still "running and kicking", so commentators criticized Hoddle: his play made the team's attack postponed, a rhythm error (like what Hamann was pointing at). from Thiago in Liverpool today). In England, Hoddle is never evaluated adequately for his talent. He went to Monaco and immediately became the best foreign player on the French pitch. He took Monaco to the championship - most likely many times to build a "dynasty", if not hindered by Marseille. Later, it was discovered that Marseille's dirty "buy-in" game during that period.

Arsene Wenger is the one who invited Hoddle to Monaco. Later, he went to Arsenal , becoming a legendary coach, teaching his home football many good lessons about football. Wenger's coaching career is closely related to his forte "looking at people", worth calling it the most skillful. Wenger saw a great tanker Hoddle, while the English people only saw that it was the player who made the team break the rhythm. And don't say Wenger doesn't like speed!

With Juergen Klopp, speed is not only the important factor. It is also philosophy. Did he not know that his Liverpool was speed-oriented, and did not see that the rhythm failure was harmful? After all, Thiago was recruited by Klopp. Just look at Virgil Van Dijk , Fabinho, Diogo Jota, see how Klopp chose the excellent person.

Football only has different ways of playing, there is no right or wrong way to play. And in top football, the distinctive style of strong teams like Liverpool, Man City , and Bayern Munich does not say that these teams cannot play the other way. Usually, teams have only one immutable play that is easy ... self-destructive. Not to mention, this is a crazy season, extremely unfavorable for speed-oriented teams (due to no corresponding preparation conditions). No wonder Liverpool kept playing "heavy metal" all the time (and succeeded forever).

The idea that Thiago will do Liverpool's attack is out of rhythm, so it's just worth ... listening for fun. The important thing here is, that opinion suggests something much better. With a special technical and creative "volleyball" like Thiago Alcantara (from Barcelona's famous La Masia youth center) in midfield, Liverpool fans have the right to wait for new features. far more engaging than the rushing gameplay, now out of date. If Thiago's habit of holding the ball before passing makes Liverpool's attack speed slow, it makes the decisive pass faster, in thought.

From winning the Champions League to winning the Premier League, Liverpool has never really had a big midfielder, comparable to Thiago Alcantara in theory. I'm glad that it's not over when I strengthened this star, I'm afraid of the attack!
